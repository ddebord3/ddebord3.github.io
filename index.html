About Me

I am a robotics engineer interested in motion planning, controls, and perception. I am driven to develop and implement new and exciting technologies in robotics. My proficiency in multiple programming languages such as a Python and C++ alongside my skills and experience in controls, computer vision, and motion planning algorithms give me the capability to work in a variety of roles. In the past I have designed and implemented computer vision software for an autonomous underwater vehicle, engineered new controls for a swarm of networked robots, among other things.

Experience

Senior Design Project: Undersea Predator Robot

Computer Vision Lead

This project developed software for an automous underwater vehicle so that it would be capable of locating and identifying sea urchins in environments where they were overpopulated. I served as the computer vision lead and programmed software that was not only capable of identifying sea urchins, but also counting and tracking them as they were detected. Using YOLOv3, the system operated successfully in real time despite strict hardware limitations such as the lack of a dedicated graphics card.

In addition to this, I developed and performed hardware tests to assess the functionality of key hardware. Code and tests were subjected to a rigorous peer review process and updated accordingly.

Georgia Tech Vertically Integrated Projects Program

TerraBots Student

As a part of a long term effort to develop autonomous construction site robots, I worked on the planning of movements around a hypothetical construction site. I programmed a variety of methods ranging from fast marching to interior-point optimization to find the optimal path to take around the worksite under the constraints and physical limitations robots would have in operation. This work was documented and passed down from student to student in the program.

Projects and Code Samples

ROS YOLOv3 Object Detection

This is a Python ROS implementation of the YOLOv3 object detection algorithm using OpenCV. Through a camera feed provided via a ROS node, it identifies objects in view of the robot. The neural network configuration and weights used may be provided by the user. It also counts and tracks objects as they are identified by the system.

View Project

Bird-oid Obstacle Avoidance Controls

This is a MATLAB implementation of a custom control algorithm for robots in the Georgia Tech Robotarium. The controls allow for networked robots to move themselves in accordance with flock collision avoidance, flock centering, and velocity matching behaviors while avoiding obstacles and other swarms.
